Chapter 1

PCOD vs PCOS

PCOD (Polycystic Ovarian Disease): A hormonal imbalance leading to immature eggs turning into cysts inside the ovaries. It is often manageable with dedicated diet and exercise changes.

PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome): A more severe metabolic disorder affecting hormones, causing irregular periods, excessive bodily hair growth, and potential fertility issues. Medical attention and specific medication are frequently recommended to keep insulin and hormones in check.

Chapter 2

Endometriosis

A chronic condition where tissue similar to the lining of the uterus grows outside the uterus.

Symptoms: Severe pelvic pain, incredibly heavy periods, pain with bowel movements or urination, and potential infertility.

It affects roughly 10% of reproductive-age women globally. Diagnosis typically takes 7-10 years due to the normalization of severe period pain in society.

Chapter 3

PMDD & Uterine Fibroids

PMDD (Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorder): A severe, debilitating extension of PMS. Causes extreme mood shifts, deep sadness, anger, and irritability that disrupt daily life drastically prior to menstruation.

Uterine Fibroids: Noncancerous growths of the uterus that often appear during childbearing years. They can vary dramatically in size and cause heavy menstrual bleeding, prolonged periods, and intense pelvic pressure.

Chapter 4

Adenomyosis

A condition where the inner lining of the uterus (the endometrium) breaks through the muscle wall of the uterus (the myometrium).

This condition can cause menstrual cramps, lower abdominal pressure, and bloating before menstrual periods and can lead to extremely heavy bleeding. It is often confused with fibroids but involves entirely different tissue dynamics.

Chapter 5

Sanitation Practices (Part 1)

  • Pads: Change every 4-6 hours. Leaving them longer allows bacteria to multiply rapidly, leading to odor and potential infections. Never flush them, as they will expand and ruin plumbing.
  • Tampons: Change every 4-8 hours to reduce TSS risk. Always start with the lowest absorbency necessary for your flow.

Chapter 5.5

Sanitation Practices (Part 2)

  • Menstrual Cups: Empty every 8-12 hours depending on flow. High-quality silicone cups can last years. Sterilize by boiling for 5-10 minutes between your cycles.
  • Period Underwear: Change every 10-12 hours. Rinse in cold water first so blood doesn't stain, then machine wash on cold without fabric softeners.
  • Always wash hands thoroughly before and after handling any menstrual product!

Chapter 6

Toxic Shock Syndrome (TSS)

A rare, life-threatening complication of certain bacterial infections (Staph/Strep). It's primarily associated with superabsorbent tampons left in for too long, creating a breeding ground for toxins.

Symptoms: Sudden high fever, low blood pressure, vomiting, muscle aches, and a rash resembling a sunburn on your palms or soles.

Chapter 7

Random Fun Facts 🧠

  • Periods can literally get worse when it's cold! Cold weather constricts blood vessels, increasing cramp pain.
  • On average, you only lose about 2-3 tablespoons of blood per entire cycle.
  • Due to hormonal fluctuations, your vocal cords actually swell—slightly changing the tone of your voice during your period!
